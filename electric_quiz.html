CALCULATING ELECTRIC POTENTIAL

Electric potential is a scalar quantity (just add the values):

1. Calculate potential from charge A: VA = kQA/rA
2. Calculate potential from charge B: VB = kQB/rB
3. Total potential: Vtotal = VA + VB

Note: Positive charges create positive potential, negative charges create negative potential.

CALCULATING FIELD STRENGTH

Field strength is a vector quantity (requires vector addition):

1. Calculate field from charge A: EA = k|QA|/rA2
2. Calculate field from charge B: EB = k|QB|/rB2
3. Find angle between the two field vectors
4. Use cosine rule: Eresultant = √(EA2 + EB2 + 2EAEBcos θ)

Fields point away from positive charges, toward negative charges.

CONSTANTS & UNITS

k = 8.99 × 109 N·m2/C2

Charges are in nC (10-9 C), distances in meters
